About Me

Welcome to my academic portfolio! I am an Assistant Professor at Haldia Institute of Technology, where I bring over 17 years of teaching experience and a passion for innovation in computer science. My academic path has been marked by rigorous study and research, having completed my master's degree from Jadavpur University and currently pursuing a PhD from National Institute of Technology.

My research and professional interests are centered around some of the most exciting and transformative areas in technology: Artificial Intelligence, Deep Learning, Data Science, and Wireless Sensor Networks. These fields represent the forefront of innovation, and I am dedicated to exploring their potential to solve real-world problems and improve our understanding of complex systems.

In my teaching career, I have had the pleasure of instructing a variety of courses, including Theory of Automata, Data Structures, Internet Technology, Machine Learning, and Algorithms. Each subject offers a unique lens through which to view the vast landscape of computer science, and I strive to make these topics accessible and engaging for my students.

I believe in the power of education to transform lives and societies. My goal as an educator is to create a dynamic and interactive learning environment where students are encouraged to think critically, collaborate, and push the boundaries of their knowledge. By fostering a culture of curiosity and innovation, I aim to prepare my students for the challenges and opportunities of the future.

My work in AI and deep learning focuses on developing intelligent systems that can learn from data and adapt to new situations. In data science, I am interested in uncovering patterns and insights from large datasets to drive decision-making and innovation. My research in wireless sensor networks aims to enhance communication and data collection in various applications, from environmental monitoring to healthcare.

Thank you for visiting my portfolio. I invite you to explore my research, publications, and projects to learn more about my work and contributions to the field of computer science.
